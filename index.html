Advanced Prompting Techniques

Break complex tasks into smaller, sequential steps.

Instead of one giant prompt, guide the AI through a multi-step process. Each step builds on the previous one, giving you more control and better results.

Example: Instead of "Create a launch plan," you would chain:
  1. "First, identify key market segments for our new product."
  2. "Then, develop targeted messaging for each of those segments."
  3. "Finally, create a detailed project timeline based on that messaging."

Ask the AI to explain its reasoning step-by-step.

Simply add the phrase "Let's think through this step by step" to your prompt. This forces the AI to show its work, which is invaluable for analysis, problem-solving, and catching logical errors early.

Example Prompt: "A user is getting error 404 on their profile page after changing their username. Let's think through this step by step to find the likely cause."

Have the AI explore multiple different approaches in parallel.

This is useful when you want to compare options instead of committing to a single path. Ask the AI to evaluate different solutions and list the pros and cons for each.

Example Prompt: "Give me three different approaches to organizing our technical documentation, with pros and cons for each."

Ask the AI to help you write a better prompt.

The AI knows what information it needs to perform a task well. You can leverage this by asking it to act as a prompt engineer.

Example Prompt: "You are an expert prompt engineer. Help me write a prompt to generate technical specifications for our API documentation."

Four Refinement Strategies

When a prompt isn't giving you what you need, try one of these four approaches.

1. Revisit TCREI

Are you missing context? Is your task specific enough? Re-check the fundamentals first.

2. Break It Down

Make the task smaller or more specific. Instead of "write a product overview," try "write a two-paragraph summary focusing on security features."

3. Try an Analogy

Rephrase your request using a different context, like "Explain this like I'm a new employee," or "Write this as if it's for our FAQ."

4. Add Constraints

Be more explicit about what you want or don't want. For example: "Keep it under 200 words," or "Avoid using technical jargon."

Remember, if a prompt fails, that's feedback. Adjust and try again.

Using AI Responsibly at Flash

With these powerful tools comes responsibility. Let's talk about using AI effectively and safely in our work environment.

Data Security

Never put sensitive or confidential information into public AI models. Use our internal tools for sensitive work.

Bias & Hallucinations

AI can be confidently wrong. Always fact-check critical outputs, especially for client-facing content or technical specifications.

Company Guidelines

Make sure you're familiar with Flash's AI usage policies. When in doubt, always ask first.

Human-in-the-Loop

Keep yourself in the review and decision-making process. AI should augment your judgment, not replace it.

Sometimes, the best decision is not to use AI - especially for high-stakes or regulated content.
