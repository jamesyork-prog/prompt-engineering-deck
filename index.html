What is a Prompt?

A prompt is the instruction or question you give to an AI system. The quality of your prompt determines the quality of your output—prompting is both an art and a science.

Prompt Example

Basic Prompt
"Write about our new product."
TCREI Prompt
"You are a technical writer at Flash. Write a brief overview of our new analytics dashboard for a client-facing knowledge base article. Focus on key benefits for enterprise users and include a clear call-to-action for scheduling a demo."
Same AI, different outputs. Context and clarity change everything.

The TCREI Framework

TCREI stands for Task, Context, References, Evaluate, and Iterate—a systematic approach for getting the best from any AI tool.

Be clear and specific about what you want the AI to do.
Provide background, audience, and constraints.
Give examples or data for more accurate output.
Review the response to ensure it meets your needs.
Refine your prompt and repeat for better results.

Task: Be Clear and Specific

What exactly do you want the AI to do? Define the role if needed and specify the format. Vague tasks get vague results—specific tasks get useful results.

Context: Set the Stage

Add background, goals, audience, and constraints. For example: “This client is a mid-size enterprise evaluating multiple solutions and values detailed technical specs.”

References: Provide Supporting Info

Give the AI examples, data, or links to help it generate more accurate and relevant outputs. The more you provide, the more tailored the response.

Evaluate: Check the Output

Review the AI’s response. Does it meet your needs? Is it accurate and actionable? If not, refine your prompt or clarify your requirements.

Iterate: Refine and Repeat

Prompting is an iterative process. Adjust your instructions, add details, or ask follow-up questions to improve the results.

Advanced Prompting Techniques

  • Chain-of-thought: Ask the AI to reason step by step.
  • Role prompting: Assign the AI a persona or expertise.
  • Multi-turn: Build on previous responses for complex tasks.

Multimodal Prompts

Modern AIs can process images, tables, and more. Combine text instructions with visuals or data for richer interactions.

Common Pitfalls

  • Being too vague or too broad
  • Forgetting to specify audience or format
  • Not checking outputs for hallucinations or errors
